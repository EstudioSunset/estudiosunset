Logo Rationale — from concept to clarity

Side‑by‑side comparison to show the reasoning behind the redesign.

Before — original mark
After — refined mark

Actual Logo

  • Typography: The “3 Bite” lettering in red feels playful, but the uneven “B” and “i” reduce legibility.
  • Mascot: The gecko appears in full body with oversized yellow eyes and tongue out. Fun and cartoonish, but a bit too childish for a premium spirit.
  • Composition: The gecko dominates the design, drawing attention away from the brand name.
  • Graphic context: The patterned Mexican frame is colorful, but it makes the design feel busy and less versatile for modern digital or small-scale uses.

Proposal

  • Typography: The green “3BITE” wordmark is bold, thick, and includes three bite marks; a clever nod to the name. Highly legible even at smaller sizes.
  • Mascot: The gecko is simplified and better integrated. Still friendly and eye-catching with bright yellow eyes, but now in balance with the text.
  • Agave element: The agave plant in the background clearly anchors the brand to mezcal without clutter. Adds authenticity and a premium feel.
  • Composition: The brand name is now the hero, with the gecko as a supporting character, improving recognition.
  • Versatility: This design adapts more effectively across bottle labels, caps, merchandise, and social media.

Brand Positioning & Voice

Essence

Tradition meets bold character. An artisanal mezcal crafted with respect for the land; and a confident visual identity built for modern discovery.

Values

  • Authenticity & origin
  • Craft & transparency
  • Community & celebration

Voice & Tone

  • Tone: warm, confident, grounded.
  • Voice: short lines, sensory words, no clichés.
  • Tagline ideas: “From agave to soul.” / “Born of smoke & sun.”

Target Audience

Persona A

The Explorer (25–40)

Urban professionals who value unique flavors, design, and cultural stories. Shops at specialty stores, follows craft spirits on IG.

Persona B

The Foodie Host

Entertains friends, seeks cocktail recipes & pairings. Will pay more for a bottle that feels like a conversation piece.

Persona C

The Bartender Ally

Local bartenders & mixologists who influence trial with signature cocktails and recommendations at the bar.

Social Foundations — Instagram & Facebook

Page setup

  • Handles aligned to brand name + location (if needed).
  • Bio with origin, tasting notes, and legal age gate.
  • Story highlights: Origin, Process, Cocktails, Events.
  • UGC permissions workflow + branded hashtag.

Content pillars

  • Craft & terroir (fields, roasting, distillation).
  • Design & culture (label details, artist collabs).
  • Cocktails & pairings (short, tight recipes).
  • People & places (mezcaleros, bars, community).

Posting cadence (starter)

  • 3 posts/week (1 craft, 1 cocktail, 1 people/culture)
  • 4–6 stories/week (BTS, polls, events, quick recipes)
  • 2 reels/month (signature serve + brand story)

Website & E‑commerce — layout & content

Recommended layout

  • Hero: bottle on textured background + 1‑line value prop + age gate link.
  • Story: origin, process (roast, mill, ferment, distill) with photos.
  • Product: tasting notes, cocktails, awards, reviews.
  • Retail: store locator or “buy online” with trusted partners.
  • Newsletter: early access to limited releases.
  • Footer: legal, sustainability, contact.

Conversion tips

  • Sticky “Buy”/"Where to Buy" on product pages.
  • UGC & expert quotes near CTAs.
  • Structured data for Product, Organization, and FAQ.
  • Fast load, compressed imagery, lazy loading.

Content to drive traffic

  • How‑to cocktail articles (rank for recipe keywords).
  • Process explainers (e.g., “What is espadín mezcal?”).
  • Event recaps with photos (signals local community).
  • Press kit page with downloadable assets.

Awareness Video — concept & structure

Concept: “From Agave to Soul”

A 30‑sec spot that travels from dawn in the agave fields to a night toast. Close‑ups of roasting, hands at work, bottle reveal, and a final clink. Minimal VO, rich sound design.

  • 0–5s: Fields at sunrise, soft percussion.
  • 6–15s: Fire, roasting, copper still, pouring into glass.
  • 16–25s: Label macro, condensation, cocktail garnish.
  • 26–30s: Friends toast. Tagline + URL/QR.

Deliverables

  • 15s cut (Stories/Reels), 30s master (IG/FB/YouTube).
  • 4:5, 9:16, 1:1 crops.
  • Captions open, safe‑area graphics, end card.
